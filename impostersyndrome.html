Imposter Syndrome Disclaimer

A warm invitation to contribute to the BSides Fort Myers conference.

How to contribute

Imposter syndrome disclaimer: The BSides Fort Myers conference wants your help. No really, it does.

There might be a little voice inside that tells you you're not ready; that you need to do one more tutorial, or learn another framework, or write a few more blog posts, or have CVE(s) attributed to you before you can attend a Security BSides conference, let alone speak at one.

I assure you, that is not the case.

This conference has a clear Code of Conduct. This Code of Conduct is there to give you confidence that no matter what mistakes you make, you'll be treated with respect. Everyone makes mistakes - that's a natural part of learning. Our pledge to you is that we are here to help you learn, not to insult or belittle you for learning.1.

Have an idea, topic, or research you want to talk about? The Call for Presentation guideline outlines the process you will need to follow to submit a presentation for consideration and the selection process. By making expectations and processes explicit, I hope it will make it easier for you to contribute to the conference!

And you don't have to have a presentation to contribute to the conference. You can help out by being a participant at the conference, asking questions, and being curious. The conference will also be seeking volunteers to help ensure the conference runs smoothly. No need for previous experience, we will either train you or pair you with another volunteer! Even giving feedback about the conference is welcome.

Thank you for contributing and being part of the conference!
